## üö® PRIORITY 1 - Critical (Add ASAP)

These substances are commonly encountered, frequently misrepresented, or pose significant harm reduction concerns.

### Opioids (5 missing)
**Why Priority:** Most deadly drug class. Fentanyl contamination common. Users need to identify pharma vs counterfeit.

- [ ] **Oxycodone (OxyContin, Percocet)** - #1 priority
  - Most commonly counterfeited pharma opioid after M30s
  - Often contains fentanyl when bought on street
  - Need: Reagent reactions, pill identification, fentanyl warning
  
- [ ] **Hydrocodone (Vicodin, Norco)** - #2 priority
  - Common prescription opioid, frequently diverted
  - Counterfeit pills increasing
  - Need: Reagent reactions, distinguish from oxy
  
- [ ] **Morphine**
  - Medical/pharmaceutical context
  - Baseline opioid (others compared to it)
  - Need: Reagent reactions, medical vs street use
  
- [ ] **Tramadol**
  - Unique opioid (SNRI properties)
  - Lower abuse potential but still diverted
  - Seizure risk at high doses
  - Need: Reagent reactions, seizure warning

### Cathinones / Bath Salts (11 missing - MAJOR GAP)
**Why Priority:** Frequently sold as MDMA, cocaine, or meth. Harm reduction essential.

- [ ] **Mephedrone (4-MMC)** - #1 cathinone priority
  - Most popular cathinone historically
  - Sold as "bath salts" or fake MDMA
  - Need: Reagent reactions, distinguish from MDMA
  
- [ ] **Alpha-PVP (Flakka)** - #2 cathinone priority
  - Extreme stimulant, psychosis risk
  - Media coverage/"zombie drug" reputation
  - Need: Reagent reactions, psychosis warning, emergency protocols
  
- [ ] **MDPV** - #3 cathinone priority
  - Original "bath salts" substance (2010-2011 crisis)
  - Extreme stimulation, paranoia, violence
  - Need: Reagent reactions, danger warnings
  
- [ ] **Methylone (bk-MDMA)**
  - Most common MDMA substitute 2012-2015
  - Still encountered
  - Need: Reagent reactions, how to distinguish from MDMA
  
- [ ] **Eutylone** / **N-Ethylpentylone**
  - Current MDMA adulterants (2020s)
  - Replacing methylone in fake molly
  - Need: Reagent reactions, current threat level
  
- [ ] **Ethylone (bk-MDEA)**
  - Another MDMA substitute
  - Less common but still encountered
  
- [ ] **Pentedrone** / **Butylone** / **NEP** / **Hexen**
  - Research chemicals, less common
  - Lower priority but should document

### Stimulants (4 missing)
**Why Priority:** Commonly prescribed ADHD meds, frequently diverted/abused by students.

- [ ] **Amphetamine (speed)** - #1 stimulant priority
  - Base substance, frequently confused with meth
  - Need: Distinguish from methamphetamine, reagent reactions
  
- [ ] **Dextroamphetamine (Dexedrine)**
  - Prescription ADHD med
  - Component of Adderall
  - Need: Pharmaceutical context, reagent reactions
  
- [ ] **Lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse)** - #2 stimulant priority
  - Most prescribed ADHD med (behind Adderall)
  - Prodrug (harder to abuse)
  - Need: How it differs from other amphetamines
  
- [ ] **Modafinil (Provigil)**
  - Wakefulness drug, cognitive enhancer
  - Popular in nootropic/student communities
  - Need: Not an amphetamine, safer profile, but still abused

## üìù Information Needed for Each Entry

When adding a new substance, include:

### Required Fields:
- [ ] **Class** (e.g., "opioid", "cathinone", "benzodiazepine")
- [ ] **Description** (overview, origins, uses, dangers)
- [ ] **Reagent test results** (Marquis, Mecke, Mandelin, Simon's, Ehrlich)
- [ ] **Forms** (powder, pill, crystal, etc.)
- [ ] **Notes** (key identification tips)
- [ ] **Links** (Wikipedia, Erowid, PsychonautWiki)

### Recommended Fields:
- [ ] **Scientific papers** (2-3 key references)
- [ ] **Common street names**
- [ ] **Adulterants/cutting agents**
- [ ] **Emergency protocols** (if relevant)
- [ ] **Legal status** (if relevant to harm reduction)

### Critical for Harm Reduction:
- [ ] **How to distinguish from similar substances** (e.g., MDMA vs cathinones)
- [ ] **Counterfeit warning** (if pharmaceutical)
- [ ] **Fentanyl contamination risk** (if relevant)
- [ ] **Unique dangers** (seizure risk, serotonin syndrome, etc.)
